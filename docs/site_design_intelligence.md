## ⚠️ INDIVIDUALITY IS NON-NEGOTIABLE

Every site must be built AROUND the specific business — not from a template.
Before writing a single line of code, run the research_business skill to collect:

### Required Inputs Per Business
- **Real logo** — scrape from their website, Facebook, Google Maps. Use it in the nav + hero.
- **Brand colors** — extract from their existing materials, signage, Google photos, FB page.
  If no colors found: derive from category + vibe (see category rules below)
- **Real fonts** — if they use a Google Font on their site, import it. Otherwise pick a font that matches their vibe.
- **Real photos** — ALL available from Google Maps (up to 12), Yelp, Facebook. Use them everywhere.
- **Real reviews** — verbatim, with author name + star count. Never paraphrase.
- **Real hours** — exact open/close times for every day. Never write "Mon-Sat 9-7" without confirming.
- **Real address** — with a clickable Google Maps deep link
- **Real phone** — clickable `tel:` link, minimum 3 places on page
- **Owner/team info** — name, background, anything found in reviews or about sections
- **Awards/badges** — "voted best", certifications, years in business, family owned, etc.
- **Taglines/copy** — pull from their existing materials, not made up
- **Social links** — Facebook, Instagram URLs if found
- **Category-specific content** — menu items for restaurants, service list for trades, etc.

### What MUST Be Unique Per Site
- Color system — derived from THEIR brand, not our preference
- Font pairing — matches their personality
- Hero layout — one of 8+ distinct layouts, never repeat across businesses
- Section order — varies by category and vibe
- Copy tone — luxury salon ≠ pressure washing company ≠ taco shop
- Logo placement and treatment
- Animation style — subtle for luxury, bold for trades, playful for food

## HERO LAYOUTS (rotate, never repeat same layout for 2 businesses)
1. **Full bleed dark photo** — text overlay left, CTA right
2. **Split screen** — left dark panel (text), right full photo
3. **Floating photo collage** — scattered polaroids over dark/colored bg
4. **Diagonal slice** — angled divider between color and photo
5. **Bento grid hero** — text + 3-4 photo tiles arranged in asymmetric grid
6. **Full bleed video bg** — text centered, video muted behind
7. **Magazine editorial** — large typography takes 70% of hero, small photo accent
8. **Before/after reveal** — drag slider (pressure washing, cleaning, auto)
9. **Layered depth** — multiple overlapping photos at different opacities
10. **Neon/glow** — dark bg, neon accent color, bold type (barbershops, tattoo, gym)

## UNIVERSAL RULES (apply to every site)

### Must-Haves
- **Click-to-call phone number** — `<a href="tel:...">` — visible on mobile above the fold, ALWAYS
- **Real Google reviews** embedded verbatim with author name and star rating — not paraphrased
- **Real photos** — use all available Google Maps photos, scrape Facebook/Instagram if needed
- **Hours displayed clearly** — exact times, every day of week
- **Address with Google Maps deep link** — `https://maps.google.com/?q=ADDRESS`
- **Mobile-first** — viewport meta, large tap targets, no tiny text
- **Sticky nav or floating CTA** — phone number always accessible
- **Hero above the fold** — name, what they do, city, CTA button — in under 3 seconds of reading
- **Logo in nav** — always, mixed with business name if logo is icon-style

### Trust Signals (use all that apply)
- Number of Google reviews ("112 verified reviews")
- Star rating displayed as actual stars ★★★★★
- Years in business if known ("Serving Evans since 2018")
- "Family owned" badge if found in research
- Guarantee language ("Satisfaction guaranteed", "We come back free")
- Named testimonials with specific details — generic quotes kill trust
- Awards or "best of" mentions if found
- Certifications, licenses, associations

### Animation & Motion
- Hover effects on all cards and buttons — scale, glow, color shift
- Scroll-triggered fade-ins on section content
- Smooth transitions on all interactive elements
- Number counters animate on scroll (years, reviews, customers served)
- Photo galleries support lightbox click-to-expand
- Sticky nav transitions from transparent to solid on scroll

## BARBERSHOP

### Best Practices (from research)
- Bold, masculine color systems: black + gold, black + red, navy + cream, dark + electric blue
- Video hero if any video content found — otherwise full dark photo
- Named barber profiles with personality ("I grew up cutting my brothers' hair in the garage")

- Embed real Google reviews, especially ones naming specific barbers

### Hero Options
- Full bleed dark photo, oversized cut-out typography
- Diagonal slice: dark side with text, photo side
- Neon glow text on pitch black bg

### Sections
1. Hero — bold, dark, cinematic
2. "Who We Are" — shop personality in 2 punchy sentences
3. Services with pricing + duration
4. Barber team profiles — real names, real photos if available
5. Reviews — embedded, real names, specific mentions

## FONT IMPORTING (via Google Fonts CDN)
Always import fonts via a `<style>` tag at top of component:
```
@import url('https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Playfair+Display:ital,wght@0,400;0,700;1,400&family=Inter:wght@300;400;600&display=swap');
```
Then use in inline styles: `fontFamily: "'Playfair Display', serif"`

Common pairings by vibe:
- Luxury/salon: Playfair Display + Inter
- Bold/barbershop: Bebas Neue + Roboto
- Modern/clean: DM Sans + DM Serif Display
- Warm/restaurant: Lora + Nunito
- Industrial/trades: Oswald + Open Sans
- Editorial/Korean: Cormorant Garamond + Helvetica Neue
- Gen Z/Vietnamese: Space Grotesk + Inter
